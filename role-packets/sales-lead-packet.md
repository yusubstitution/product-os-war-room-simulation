### TRANSCRIPT #2: TechConf Post-Mortem (Lost Deal)

**Prospect:** TechConf (Annual Developer Conference Organizer)
**Deal Size:** $500,000 ACV (3-year contract)
**Segment:** Mid-Market
**Date:** December 18, 2025, 2:00 PM PT
**Attendees:**
- Kevin Walsh, CEO & Founder (TechConf)
- Amanda Foster, Account Executive (EventFlow)
- Mark Chen, Sales Engineer (EventFlow)

**Context:** Kevin has decided to go with Bizzabo after 6-week evaluation. This is a post-mortem call to understand why we lost.

---

**Amanda Foster:** Kevin, first of all, thank you for taking the time to give us feedback. I know you've probably been through the wringer with demos and evaluations. We really appreciate you being willing to chat even though you've made your decision.

**Kevin Walsh:** Of course, Amanda. I want to be helpful because you guys were great to work with. This was a tough decision - you were our second choice.

**Mark Chen:** That's good to hear, at least. Can you walk us through what ultimately tipped the scales toward Bizzabo?

**Kevin:** Sure. So, context: TechConf is a 3-day developer conference with about 2,000 attendees. We have 80 speakers across 6 tracks, lots of networking events, sponsor booths, the whole thing. Networking is absolutely core to the experience - people come to TechConf to meet other developers, potential employers, investors, etc.

**Amanda:** Right, I remember you emphasized that in our first call.

**Kevin:** Yeah, so when we were comparing EventFlow and Bizzabo, the networking features were a major differentiator. Bizzabo has this AI-powered matchmaking where attendees fill out their interests and job roles, and the system suggests people they should meet. They also have a 1:1 meeting scheduler built in where people can book 15-minute coffee chats, and it syncs with their Google Calendar.

**Mark:** We do have some networking features - attendees can view each other's profiles, there's a discussion board, direct messaging...

**Kevin:** I know, Mark, and those are fine. But they're pretty basic. With Bizzabo's AI matchmaking, attendees get push notifications like "You should meet Jane - you both work in ML infrastructure." That's powerful for a conference where networking is the main value prop.

**Amanda:** That makes sense. Were there other factors or was networking the primary driver?

**Kevin:** Networking was the biggest thing, but there were a few smaller issues. Honestly, Amanda, your registration flow on mobile was pretty clunky compared to Bizzabo's. I tested both on my phone and Bizzabo's was much smoother - fewer form fields, payment was easier. I didn't think much of it at first, but then I realized 65% of our registrations come from mobile, especially the younger developers. So that matters.

**Mark:** Interesting. I hadn't realized mobile was such a high percentage of your traffic.

**Kevin:** Yeah, most developer conferences see high mobile registration because people are browsing Twitter or Reddit on their phones, see the conference announcement, and register right away. If the mobile experience is friction-y, you lose them.

**Amanda:** That's really valuable feedback. Anything else that influenced the decision?

**Kevin:** Hmm, let me think. Oh, one thing that would have been nice - I spent like 3 hours setting up the event page, schedule, speaker bios, all of that. With Bizzabo, I could just clone last year's TechConf event and update the speakers and dates. That would have saved me a ton of time. You guys didn't have that feature.

**Mark:** Event cloning is on our roadmap, but you're right, we don't have it today. Was that a major factor?

**Kevin:** Not major, but it contributed to the overall impression that Bizzabo was more polished and mature. Like, I'm running the same conference every year with minor tweaks - why am I rebuilding everything from scratch?

**Amanda:** Totally fair. Kevin, if we had the networking features, would that have changed your decision?

**Kevin:** Hard to say for sure, but probably yes. The networking piece was the main gap. Everything else was pretty comparable - your pricing was actually a bit better than Bizzabo's. But for TechConf, networking features are table stakes.

**Mark:** Got it. This is super helpful, Kevin. One last question - do you think the networking features are important for all types of events, or specifically for conferences like TechConf?

**Kevin:** Good question. I think it's really important for multi-day conferences where networking is a primary goal. If I were running, like, a webinar series or a single-day workshop, I probably wouldn't care as much. But for TechConf, it's essential.

**Amanda:** That makes sense. Well, Kevin, thank you for the honesty. We're going to take this feedback back to our product team. Even though we didn't win this deal, I hope we can stay in touch. Maybe we'll have a chance to work together in the future.

**Kevin:** Absolutely. And hey, if you guys build out those networking features, let me know. I'm committed to Bizzabo for the next year, but we evaluate vendors annually.

**Amanda:** Will do. Thanks again, Kevin.

**[Call ends]**

---

**Amanda's Internal Notes:**
- Lost $500K deal primarily due to networking features (AI matchmaking, meeting scheduler)

- Secondary issues: Mobile registration was "clunky" (65% of his registrations are mobile), lack of event cloning (spent 3 hours on manual setup)
- Pricing was competitive - this was a product gap, not a pricing issue
- Note: Kevin said networking is critical for conferences, less important for webinars/workshops

### TRANSCRIPT #3: InnovateCorp Expansion Opportunity

**Customer:** InnovateCorp (SaaS Marketing Platform)
**Current ACV:** $150,000/year (20 events/year)
**Expansion Opportunity:** $400,000/year (60 events/year)
**Segment:** Mid-Market
**Date:** February 5, 2026, 10:00 AM PT
**Attendees:**
- David Park, Chief Marketing Officer (InnovateCorp)
- Rachel Kim, Account Executive (EventFlow)

**Context:** InnovateCorp is scaling their webinar program from 20 to 60 events per year. This is a major expansion opportunity.

---

**Rachel Kim:** David, congratulations on the funding round! I saw the TechCrunch article. Series C, $80 million - that's huge.

**David Park:** Thank you! Yeah, we're excited. It's going to let us scale the marketing team significantly. That's actually what I wanted to talk to you about.

**Rachel:** I figured this was related. What's the plan?

**David:** So, we're currently doing about 20 webinars a year - mostly demand gen and product education. With the new funding, we're scaling to 60 webinars per year. We're hiring three more content marketers, and each person will run a weekly webinar series.

**Rachel:** That's incredible growth. 60 webinars is a serious operation.

**David:** Yeah, which is why I'm thinking about our event platform strategy. EventFlow has been great for our current needs, but I need to make sure we have the right tools to scale 3x.

**Rachel:** Absolutely. What are your biggest concerns about scaling?

**David:** Well, first question - can EventFlow handle 60 events per year from a technical standpoint? I don't want to hit any limits.

**Rachel:** Definitely. We have customers running hundreds of events per year on EventFlow. That's not a problem at all from a capacity perspective.

**David:** Good, good. The other thing is... okay, so our current process is that our content marketer spends like 2-3 hours setting up each webinar event page, writing the description, setting up emails, configuring everything. If we're doing 60 events a year, that's 120-180 hours of just event setup. That's basically a full-time job.

**Rachel:** Right, that's a lot of operational overhead.

**David:** Exactly. So I've been looking at what features could help us streamline this. One thing I'm seeing from your competitors - Zoom Events, Luma, even Eventbrite - is AI-powered content generation. Like, the AI writes your session description, your promotional emails, social posts, all of that. You just give it some bullet points and it generates the content.

**Rachel:** That's interesting. Is that something you'd use?

**David:** I mean, it sounds appealing. We run the same webinar format every week - "Best Practices for [Topic]" - so it's pretty templated. If AI could generate the copy, that could save a ton of time. Plus, it's good for positioning. My CEO keeps asking if we're using "AI-forward" tools.

**Rachel:** [laughs] Every CEO is asking that these days. What else are you looking for?

**David:** Well, another thing - and this might be more realistic than AI - is just... can we template the webinars? Like, we're running the same format every week. Can I set up a template once with all the settings, branding, email sequence, everything, and then just duplicate it each week and change the title and date?

**Rachel:** That's a really good question. We don't have a formal template or cloning feature today. You'd need to set up each webinar individually.

**David:** Ugh, that's what I was afraid of. That's a lot of manual work for my team. I know Zoom Events has something like this - you can save a webinar as a template and reuse it.

**Rachel:** I hear you. Let me take that back to product as feedback. That seems like a pretty common need for customers running high-frequency events like you.

**David:** Yeah, I'd love that. I mean, between templating and AI generation, I'd probably take templating first. That's a concrete time-saver. The AI thing is more of a "nice to have" - I'd want to make sure the AI understands our brand voice. We're pretty particular about our messaging.

**Rachel:** Makes sense. What else is on your wishlist?

**David:** Okay, last thing - and this is more strategic. I need to prove ROI to my CFO. We're spending $150K on EventFlow, and if we go to $400K, I need to show that these webinars are actually driving pipeline. Right now, your analytics are pretty basic - I can see how many people attended, but I can't really show the full picture of engagement and impact.

**Rachel:** What kind of analytics would be helpful?

**David:** I want to see things like engagement scores for each attendee - who was actually paying attention vs. who was multitasking. I want to track the journey from registration to attendance to follow-up. I want an ROI calculator that shows how many opportunities came from webinars. Basically, I need to prove to my CFO that webinars are worth the investment.

**Rachel:** That's really valuable feedback. I know our analytics are somewhat limited today. Is that a blocker for the expansion, or more of a nice-to-have?

**David:** It's not a blocker for the next six months, but it will be long-term. My CFO is already asking questions like "Why are we spending $150K on webinars?" If I'm going to ask for $400K, I need better data to justify it. Otherwise, she'll tell me to just use Zoom webinars for free.

**Rachel:** Totally understand. Let me summarize what I'm hearing: event templating is the biggest operational need for scaling, better analytics are critical for proving ROI long-term, and AI content generation is a nice-to-have but not essential. Is that right?

**David:** Yeah, that's about right. Oh, and the AI stuff matters for perception too. Like I said, my CEO keeps asking if we're investing in AI tools. It's kind of a checkbox for him - he wants to feel like we're using cutting-edge tech.

**Rachel:** Got it. So AI has strategic value even if the practical value is uncertain.

**David:** Exactly. It's good for board slides. [laughs]

**Rachel:** [laughs] I understand completely. Okay, David, let me take this back to our team. We definitely want to support your expansion. Let me see what I can find out about roadmap for templating, AI, and analytics.

**David:** Sounds good. One more thing - when you talk to your product team, can you ask them about the AI roadmap specifically? I'm getting pressure from my CEO to make sure we're investing in "AI-forward" platforms. I need to be able to tell him EventFlow has AI capabilities.

**Rachel:** Absolutely. I'll ask about that specifically and get back to you next week.

**David:** Perfect. Thanks, Rachel.

**[Call ends]**
